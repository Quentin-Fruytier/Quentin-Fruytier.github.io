Qwent-Finance

Self-hosted · Python / Flask · Chronos-2 (GPU) · Gemini AI council · yfinance / FRED

Qwent-Finance is a fully self-hosted portfolio-analytics workbench I built to answer a simple question honestly: can a modern zero-shot time-series foundation model say anything useful about my holdings? It pairs a local stock-trend visualizer with Chronos-2 probabilistic “second opinions,” a rigorously backtested findings tab, and an AI investment council that deliberates over the live signals. Everything runs on my own machine on my own GPU — no data ever leaves the box, and it's decidedly not financial advice.

Screenshot — Forecast tab: Chronos-2 cone + vol/trend overlays

Why it's interesting. Two threads I care about meet here: self-hosting (keeping private financial data local, running a 120M-param forecaster on a consumer RTX GPU, and wiring it into my own AI workspace) and epistemic honesty in quantitative finance (every backtested claim carries a verdict, and the headline conclusion is a negative result I trust more than a positive one I don't).

What it does.

  • Forecast — price history with a calibrated Chronos-2 forecast cone, plus a unified overlay menu (SMA 50/200, volatility, momentum, two vol-regime shadings, and macro-context series from FRED), and an editable allocation builder with expected returns per horizon.
  • Hottest — a sortable watchlist of ~28 tickers: annualized volatility flags, Chronos / momentum / moving-average columns per horizon, and trailing 1w/1m/3m moves.
  • Performance — every backtested finding with an explicit verdict (this is where the honesty lives), alongside the live allocation and buy/sell-map panels.
  • Advisor Backtest & My Portfolio — drop in your own trade history to get a FIFO realized/unrealized P&L breakdown and a no-look-ahead “would the model have agreed with my trades?” check.
  • Council — an AI investment committee (a Quant on the Chronos signals, a web-grounded Researcher, a Risk skeptic, and a CIO Judge) deliberates and issues a ≤10-position target allocation, with a decision log and a retrospective learning loop. It runs natively on Gemini — no external workspace required.
Screenshot — Council tab: deliberation + allocation timeline

The honest bottom line. Across an adversarially-verified experiment suite, zero-shot Chronos-2 is a well-calibrated cone and a direction/conviction gauge — not an alpha source. It roughly matches 6-month momentum, has no cross-sectional skill for which asset to buy, and shows no profitable sell signal; equal-weight allocation wins on a risk-adjusted basis. The one robust, shippable payoff is a volatility-plus-trend position-sizing overlay that meaningfully cuts maximum drawdown for a modest Sharpe lift. (A clean forward test through a real post-2025 drawdown is the honest evaluation still outstanding.) I'm writing these findings up as a short manuscript.

Stack & self-hosting. Python + Flask serve the app locally; Chronos-2 runs on a consumer NVIDIA GPU (~20 ms/forecast, CPU fallback); market data comes from yfinance and macro series from FRED; the council uses the Gemini API (with optional local Ollama / Groq routing). It optionally embeds inside my self-hosted AI workspace via an iframe, but the full app — council included — runs entirely on its own.
